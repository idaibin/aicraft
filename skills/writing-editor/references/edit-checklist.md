# Edit Checklist

## Source and Integrity

Use `fact-integrity.md` as the normative checklist; do not recreate its claim inventory here.

- [ ] The source ledger, source precedence, claim trace, protected text, and unknowns pass the Fact Integrity contract.
- [ ] No mode, title, summary, shortening, or platform adaptation changes claim scope, certainty, attribution, relationship disclosure, or required process disclosure.
- [ ] Current external facts and platform rules are verified from appropriate sources, or the affected claim is marked `Not verified`, omitted, or returned as `Not enough context`.
- [ ] Already-published material that changed materially passes `revision-transparency.md`.

## Reader and Structure

- [ ] The first useful paragraph arrives immediately.
- [ ] The article has one clear reader and purpose.
- [ ] The structure follows the actual task, argument, or decision chain.
- [ ] Each section adds new information.
- [ ] Repeated previews, mini-summaries, and final recaps are removed.
- [ ] The ending completes the article without forced uplift.

## Voice

- [ ] The result sounds compatible with the author's sample or default profile.
- [ ] Genuine uncertainty, tradeoffs, and rejected alternatives remain.
- [ ] First person appears only where the source supports it.
- [ ] Sentence and paragraph rhythm varies with content.
- [ ] No fake slang, fragments, confessions, or rhetorical hooks were added.

## Anti-Template Pass

- [ ] AI-pattern decisions were based on clusters and sentence function.
- [ ] Empty openings and abstract value claims were replaced by actual context.
- [ ] Vague authority and unsupported social proof were removed.
- [ ] Mechanical three-part structures and staged punchlines were reduced.
- [ ] Formatting is functional, not decorative.
- [ ] Chatbot residue is absent.

## Mode-Specific

- [ ] Short-form contains one point and one useful detail without merging differently scoped claims or dropping source attribution.
- [ ] Soft copy persuades through mechanism and evidence, not hype, and discloses material interest when relevant.
- [ ] Performance, price, reliability, and comparison claims name the operation, baseline, sample, environment, source, source interest, and independence status.
- [ ] Tutorials include prerequisites, steps, verification, tested scope, and labels for documented-only or source-reported behavior.
- [ ] Retrospectives explain the decision and cost, not only the result.
- [ ] Platform adaptation changes shape without changing facts, terminology, metric scope, source attribution, material-interest disclosure, required creation-process disclosure, or required correction notice.

## Quality Gate

- [ ] No blocking integrity defect remains.
- [ ] No P0 or P1 editorial defect remains under `quality-rubric.md`.
- [ ] Any remaining P2 or P3 item is visible and acceptable for the requested artifact.
- [ ] The final output contains only the requested artifact.
